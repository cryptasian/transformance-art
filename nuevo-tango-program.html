Program Notes

Astor Piazzolla (1921-1992) is widely recognized as the greatest composer and ambassador of tango music, who made it his life mission to bring the signature music genre of Argentina from the dance floors onto the concert stages of Europe and America. His works revolutionized the traditional tango into a new style of Nuevo Tango, a new musical style that incorporated classical music with its technicality and musical structure, and jazz, with its unique syncopations and improvisatory elements. This musical fusion reflects Piazzolla’s life story; born in Argentina in 1921, he spent his early childhood in Buenos Aires, where he heard the old tango style played in the streets; as a teenager, his family lived in New York City, where he heard the top jazz musicians of the 1930s, and as an adult, he studied classical composition in Paris with Nadia Boulanger, who encouraged him to remain loyal to his roots in the tango style. Piazzolla created a completely new genre by merging tango, jazz, and classical music together. He was severely criticized and even death threats for his tango reforms in the 1960s. But the rest of the world embraced Piazzolla’s works. His new tango music became very popular in Europe and America among audiences and performers. His tangos are vastly transcribed for various formations and orchestras.

Histoire du Tango (History of Tango) was originally composed for flute and guitar in 1986 to convey the history and evolution of the tango in four movements. KnoxTrio will be playing two movements. “Cafe 1930” is slower and melancholy, reflecting the public’s shift from dancing to listening. “Nightclub 1960” combines the tango with the bossa nova genre that was popular at that time.

Escualo (Shark) was composed in 1979 for Piazzolla’s colleague and tango violinist Fernando Suarez Paz. This short and energetic piece is an homage to Piazzolla’s favorite hobby– fishing in Uruguay.

Oblivion was composed in Rome in 1982 for a soundtrack of Mario Bellochio’s film Enrico IV. It is in the style of Milonga, a style of music that heavily influenced Tango. It is one of Piazzolla’s most well-known, achingly beautiful, and haunting compositions.

Libertango is one of Piazzolla’s most popular masterpieces. It has been arranged for countless instruments and combinations. The title of this piece symbolizes his liberation from the traditional tango style.

Four Seasons of Buenos Aires, inspired by the Four Seasons of Vivaldi, composed over a six-year period (1965-70) is a set of four tango compositions, originally written for quintet and bandoneon. Each tango has a unique blend of Bach, Romantic, Impressionistic & Jazz. “Autumn” is the busy time of the year, “Winter”, with its quietness and desolation, “Spring” is a lively fugue, and “Summer” is outdoor music seasoned with street noises.

Performers

KnoxTrio

Joshua Ulrich is a violinist and violist who has performed internationally as a soloist, recitalist, and chamber musician. His growing artistic reputation is supported by having collaborated with such esteemed artists as James Tocco, Awadagin Pratt, and Anton Nel. He was educated at the Juilliard School and the College-Conservatory of Music at the University of Cincinnati. Joshua’s teachers included renowned pedagogues: Dorothy Delay, Won-Bin Yim, Edward Dusinberre, Geraldine Walther, Hyo Kang, Joel Smirnoff, and Cho-Liang Lin and he has performed in master classes taught by Midori, Pinchas Zuckerman, Cheeyun Kim, and Igor Ozim. He has been awarded fellowships at the Aspen Music Festival and Bowdoin. International Music Festival and was a Shouse Artist at the Great Lakes Chamber Music Festival. As an orchestral musician, Joshua was a performer with the Cincinnati Symphony Orchestra, an associate musician with the Columbus Symphony Orchestra, and the Associate Concertmaster of the Richmond Symphony Orchestra, in Indiana. Joshua has just completed a postgraduate degree at the University of Colorado-Boulder, where he and the Altius Quartet were the Fellowship String Quartet-in-Residence working with the Takács Quartet.

Sarah Senn is a new core member of the Knoxville Symphony Orchestra and is a recent graduate of the Cleveland Institute of Music in Cleveland, OH. Sarah obtained her Undergraduate Performance degree, Master’s Performance degree, and a minor in Suzuki Pedagogy at CIM under the tutelage of Dr. Melissa Kraut. Originally from Long Island, NY, Sarah began studies at the age of four and later trained in the Precollege division at the Manhattan School of Music. As a soloist, Sarah won first prize in the 2017 Tennessee Cello Workshop Competition and in the 2019 “Getting To Carnegie” competition, where she not only had the opportunity to perform in Carnegie Hall but also won a trip to the US Virgin Islands to give a concert tour. As an avid chamber music performer, Sarah has performed concerts as part of the Nashville Chamber Music Society and the Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center’s Young Artist Program and has performed alongside world-renowned musicians such as Jaime Laredo, Sharon Robinson, Joe Burgstaller, Julian Gargiulo, Zuill Bailey, Shannon Lee, Sara Daneshpour, and more. Sarah just recently got married to her musical (and handsome!) husband, Cole, and bought a home together in West Knoxville.

Belle Toh is a collaborative pianist, piano instructor, dancer, and the founder of Transformance Art. She has been teaching piano since 1983 in Malaysia, London, China, and America. She studied with professors at the Royal Academy of Music, Trinity College, and Guildhall School of Music in London and also received professional training in performance, accompaniment, and pedagogy in Malaysia, England, Arizona, Knoxville, and Berlin. Belle acquired her Bachelor's degree in Piano Performance at Northern Arizona University and her Master’s degree in Collaborative Piano at the University of Tennessee. She has performed and collaborated around the world since 1987 - playing piano for song recitals, choirs, chamber music, live TV vocal competitions, and music festivals, and has coordinated many multicultural music events. Belle has lived in 5 countries and traveled to 15. Her rich cultural heritage and exposure to other traditions during her travels have contributed to her love for playing music that incorporates classical music with elements of jazz, folk music, and other styles - with a focus on living composers. She is currently living in Knoxville, TN, teaching piano full-time, ballroom dancing, and starting a Music & Arts nonprofit to rejuvenate the souls of professional artists while bringing the healing and therapeutic benefits of the arts to the people in our community who need it.

Dancers

Kris Hazard, owner and artistic director of Marble City Ballroom, is a seasoned dancer and instructor, with over two decades of experience in the industry. He attended the University of North Carolina School of the Arts as a ballet major and received a BFA in Music Theatre from Elon University. He has performed in the Broadway national tour of The Wizard of Oz, regional theaters around the country, and locally at Dolly Parton’s Stampede and the Smoky Mountain Opry. He has instructed and choreographed for Fred Astaire Dance Studios, multiple independent studios, and Dance with Me Studios owned by DWTS alumni Tony Dovolani, and Max and Val Chmerkovsky. His passion for dance inspired him to establish Marble City Ballroom here in Knoxville. As the owner and artistic director, Kris brings a wealth of knowledge and creativity to the studio, helping students of all levels achieve their dance goals. His leadership and dedication have made Marble City Ballroom a vibrant and welcoming community for dancers of all ages and backgrounds.

Marble City Ballroom is a dynamic dance instruction and performance company in the Knoxville metro area. MCB offers professional private instruction, group classes, choreography, performances, and social dances. From wedding dances and social dancing to competitions and shows, there are many opportunities to explore your passion for dance and be a part of an open and fun-loving community of dancers. Styles include Foxtrot, Waltz, Tango, Rumba, Cha-Cha, Swing, Salsa, and many other partnership dance styles. Check out their website, and follow them on Facebook and Instagram.
